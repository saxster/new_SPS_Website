## 1. The Strunk & White Commandments

These five principles are embedded into all Agent prompts and must be enforced at every stage.

### I. Omit Needless Words
> A sentence should contain no unnecessary words, a paragraph no unnecessary sentences.

| âŒ Verbose | âœ… Concise |
|-----------|-----------|
| "In the event that a breach occurs" | "If a breach occurs" |
| "Due to the fact that" | "Because" |
| "At this point in time" | "Now" |
| "In order to" | "To" |
| "The question as to whether" | "Whether" |
| "It is important to note that" | [Delete entirely] |
| "Needless to say" | [Delete entirely] |

### II. Use the Active Voice
> The active voice is usually more direct and vigorous than the passive.

| âŒ Passive | âœ… Active |
|-----------|-----------|
| "The breach was detected by the system" | "The system detected the breach" |
| "Training should be conducted by managers" | "Managers should conduct training" |
| "It was decided that..." | "The board decided that..." |

**Exception**: Use passive when the actor is unknown or irrelevant ("Three servers were compromised").

### III. Put Statements in Positive Form
> Make definite assertions. Avoid tame, colourless, hesitating, non-committal language.

| âŒ Negative/Hedged | âœ… Positive/Direct |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| "He did not remember" | "He forgot" |
| "It is not unlikely that..." | "It is likely that..." |
| "We do not have evidence to suggest" | "Evidence is lacking" |
| "This may potentially cause issues" | "This risks causing issues" |

### IV. Use Specific, Concrete Language
> Prefer the specific to the general, the definite to the vague, the concrete to the abstract.

| âŒ Vague | âœ… Specific |
|---------|------------|
| "A significant number of incidents" | "147 incidents in Q3 2025" |

| "The cost is substantial" | "The cost ranges from â‚¹5â€“15 lakh" |

### V. Place Emphatic Words at the End
> The end of a sentence is the position of emphasis.

| âŒ Weak ending | âœ… Strong ending |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "Compliance is what organisations must prioritise today" | "Today, organisations must prioritise compliance" |

## 3. Article Archetypes

Every article must follow one of these four structural templates:

### Type A: How-To Guide (Target: 2,000+ words)
```
1. HOOK (50 words): Anecdote or startling fact
2. WHY THIS MATTERS (150 words): Stakes for the reader
3. BACKGROUND (300 words): Necessary context, definitions
4. THE STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION (1,000 words):
   - 5-8 major steps
   - Each step: What â†’ Why â†’ How â†’ Pitfalls â†’ Expert Tip
5. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID (200 words)
6. ADVANCED CONSIDERATIONS (150 words)
7. CONCLUSION WITH CALL TO ACTION (100 words)
```

### Type B: Analysis/Deep Dive (Target: 2,500+ words)
```
1. PROVOCATIVE OPENING (100 words): Challenge conventional wisdom
2. CENTRAL THESIS (50 words): One clear argument
3. EVIDENCE BLOCK 1 (400 words): Data + Interpretation
4. EVIDENCE BLOCK 2 (400 words): Case Study
5. EVIDENCE BLOCK 3 (400 words): Expert Validation
6. COUNTER-ARGUMENTS (300 words): Steel-man the opposition
7. SYNTHESIS (200 words): Reconcile tensions
8. IMPLICATIONS AND FORECAST (150 words)
```

### Type C: News Report (Target: 800+ words)
```
1. LEAD (75 words): Who, What, When, Where, Why, How
2. IMPORTANCE (100 words): Why this matters NOW
3. BACKGROUND (200 words): Necessary context
4. OFFICIAL RESPONSE (150 words): Government/Industry quotes
5. IMPACT ANALYSIS (200 words): Who benefits, who loses
6. WHAT HAPPENS NEXT (75 words)
```

### Type D: Review (Target: 1,500+ words)
```
1. OVERVIEW (150 words): What is being reviewed, for whom
2. METHODOLOGY (100 words): How we evaluated
3. KEY FINDINGS (600 words): 4-6 major points
4. VERDICT (200 words): Clear recommendation
5. ALTERNATIVES (150 words): What else to consider
6. SCORING (100 words): Rubric and final score
```

## 8. Tone Principles

### We Are
- **Authoritative**: We state facts, not guesses. Active voice default.
- **Professional**: No slang, no emojis (except in UI signals).
- **Reassuring**: Security is about safety. Convey stability and control.
- **Precise**: Numbers over adjectives. Dates over "recently".

### We Are Not
- Casual or chatty
- Sensationalist or fear-mongering
- Promotional or salesy
- Vague or hedging
